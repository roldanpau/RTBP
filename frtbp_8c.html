Derivative of the flow of the RTBP.

Compute the derivative of the flow with respect to $x$ of the RTBP for a given time and initial condition. The time $t$ may be positive or negative, allowing for forward or backward integration. This is done by numerically integrating the variational equations.

Parameters:
[in]mu_locmass parameter for the RTBP
[in]t1integration time
[in]xArgument of the derivative, 4 coordinates:
[out]dphiOn return of this function, it holds the derivative with respect to $x$.
Returns:
a non-zero error code to indicate an error and 0 to indicate success.
Remarks:
Backwards integration of the variational equations is used to obtain the derivative of the inverse Poincare map (see dprtbp).
A Taylor method (provided by Angel Jorba) is used to solve the variational equations.
We follow the convention to place the large mass (Sun) to the LEFT of the origin, and the small mass (Jupiter) to the RIGHT.

Flow of the Restricted Three Body Problem

Compute the flow $(t,x)$ of the RTBP for a given time and initial condition. The time $t$ may be positive or negative, allowing for forward or backward integration. The trajectory is integrated numerically.

Parameters:
[in]mumass parameter for the RTBP
[in]t1integration time
[in,out]x[DIM]Initial condition, 4 coordinates: On return of this function, it holds the final point $(t,x)$.
Returns:
a non-zero error code to indicate an error and 0 to indicate success. If an integration error is encountered, the function returns a non-zero value.
Remarks:
A Taylor method (provided by Angel Jorba) is used to solve the ODE.
We follow the convention to place the large mass (Sun) to the LEFT of the origin, and the small mass (Jupiter) to the RIGHT.
