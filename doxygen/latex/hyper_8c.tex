Hyperbolic Splitting (Eigenvalues/vects) Associated to a Fixed Point.

Consider a 2D map \$F\$ and its derivative \$DF\$. Given a hyperbolic fixed point \$p\$ of \$F\$, compute its hyperbolic splitting, i.e. its unstable/stable eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Since \$DF\$ is a 2x2 matrix, we compute this simply by hand, see for instance \href{http://www.math.harvard.edu/archive/21b_fall_04/exhibits/2dmatrices/index.html}{\tt http://www.math.harvard.edu/archive/21b\_\-fall\_\-04/exhibits/2dmatrices/index.html}

\begin{DoxyRemark}{Remarks}
Eigenvectors are normalised to unit magnitude.

Eigenvectors are chosen to point \char`\"{}to the right\char`\"{} of the fixed point, i.e. \$v=(x,p\_\-x)\$ with first component \$x$>$0\$.
\end{DoxyRemark}
