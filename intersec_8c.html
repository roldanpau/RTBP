Intersection of unstable invariant manifold with symmetry line.

Consider the 2D map , which is assumed to be reversible with respect to the symmetry line $p_x=0$. Let $p$ be a hyperbolic fixed point for . For definiteness, we assume that $p$ is located above the $p_x=0$ axis.

Assume that is the unstable eigenvalue, with . Let $v$ be the unstable eigenvector for the eigenvalue . For definiteness, we assume that $v=(x,p_x)$ points "to the right", i.e. we assume that the first component of $v$ is $x>0$, but we could use the other branch of the manifold. Let $W^u(p)$ be the unstable manifold of $p$. Let $p_x=l$ be a line parallel to the $x$ axis.

This function computes the "first" intersection point $z=(x,l)$ of the unstable manifold with the line $p_x=l$ as we grow the manifold from the fixed point. It does so by refining the approximate homoclinic point obtained in approxint_unst.

We consider the $n$-th iteration under the iterated Poincare map of the fundamental segment between the two points $p+h v$ and $P(p+h v)$. Let $p_u = p+h_u v$ be a point in the unstable segment. We are provided (by the program approxint) with an interval $(h_1,h_2)$ that is guaranteed to contain the root $h^*$ such that

Then we use a standard numerical method (bisection-like 1-dimensional root finding) to find $h^*$. The function that we solve (find a zero of) is distance(h_u) = l - p_x(P^n(p+h_u v_u)).

Remarks:
We ask for precision of BISECT_TOL in the homoclinc point.
